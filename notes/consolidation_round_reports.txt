VERIFIED — snap_angles Clifford preservation: round mode preserves all multiples of pi/2 exactly for delta = pi/2/{1,2,3,4,5} (30-gate random Clifford-angle circuits, diff < 1e-12); stochastic mode: 200 seeds x 13 Clifford angles at delta=pi/8 produced 0 non-Clifford outputs; delta=pi/2 snap gives rotation_census non-Clifford = 0/28. rotation_census handles negative angles correctly (Python % is non-negative).

VERIFIED — stochastic unbiasedness: E[snapped angle] = theta by construction (floor + Bernoulli(frac)); empirically, 5 independent 40k-seed blocks at theta=-1.3, delta=0.3 give biases {+2.54, -0.82, -1.48, +1.03, +0.97} sigma — consistent with zero. (Note: testing several thetas with the SAME seed range gives correlated deviations — the original-looking "2.5 sigma at two angles" is one shared fluctuation, not bias.)

VERIFIED (attacked hard) — gridsynth word-matrix convention: definitive test against pygridsynth's own matrix (gridsynth_circuit(...).to_complex_matrix(1)) shows _word_matrix's left-to-right product matches pygridsynth to ~1e-7 (float/metric noise) while the REVERSED word differs at O(eps). So pygridsynth's to_simple_str emits gates in MATRIX-PRODUCT order (leftmost char = leftmost factor = applied LAST), and the code's unreversed product is correct. The "try both orientations, pick closer" step CANNOT mask a systematic: every generator in the H/S/T/X/Z/W/I alphabet is a symmetric matrix, so word reversal = exact transpose (verified max|U_rev - U^T| = 6.7e-16), and the Rz target is diagonal (transpose-invariant), so BOTH orientations are within eps of Rz; _phase_dist ties to 1e-14..1e-9 and the <= tie-break always selects the correct forward product. No unknown characters appeared in any emitted word (alphabet fully covered by _GATES).
FINDING (LOW, documentation): the _word_matrix docstring (synthesis.py:82-86) states the opposite of what the code does ("emits in circuit order... product runs right-to-left over the string reversed" — the code does NOT reverse, and unreversed is correct). Fix: rewrite the docstring to "pygridsynth emits the word in matrix-product order (leftmost gate applied last); multiply left-to-right."

VERIFIED — global-phase W: W = e^{i pi/4} I matches pygridsynth w_phase() = pi/4; residual global phase is harmless for uncontrolled UnitaryGate insertion, and the raw (phase-included) operator-norm error of the word was <= eps in all tests.

VERIFIED — rx/ry conjugations: identities Rx = H Rz H and Ry = (S H) Rz (H S†) are exact; numerically, single-gate conjugated approximants at eps=1e-8 sit at the metric floor (~1.3e-7, see below), and a full mixed rx/ry/rz/cx circuit through gridsynth_substitute matches the exact circuit to 8.2e-4 / 9.3e-6 / 2.8e-7 at eps = 1e-3 / 1e-5 / 1e-7 (budget 6*eps) — no basis-convention error.

VERIFIED — _phase_dist vs pygridsynth's eps: for eps >= 1e-6, _phase_dist equals the phase-optimized operator-norm distance to 3 digits (ratio 1.000) and pygridsynth's guarantee holds in TRUE operator norm with margin (e.g., 4.4e-3 vs eps=1e-2, 4.0e-5 vs 1e-4). The metric is operator-norm-equivalent to O(d^2) for small d.
FINDING (MINOR, future-proofing): _phase_dist = sqrt(1-|tr/2|^2) has a float64 cancellation floor ~1e-8: a 1e-9 perturbation reads as 0.0, and near eps=1e-7 it OVERESTIMATES (true opnorm 4.6e-8 read as 1.27e-7 at theta=0.4488). Consequence: the pilot's assert d < eps (scripts/synthesis_pilot_ns8.py:95) will spuriously fail for eps <~ 2e-7. Current production tiers (1e-2..1e-4) are unaffected. One-line fix: compute the two eigenphases of U†V (np.linalg.eigvals) and use |sin(dphi/2)|.

FINDING (LOW, latent): snap_angles and gridsynth_substitute build QuantumCircuit(circ.num_qubits) only — classical bits/measurements are dropped and a measured circuit CRASHES (confirmed IndexError); circ.global_phase is also silently dropped. No current impact (production pipelines are statevector/MPS, measurement-free). Fix: QuantumCircuit(circ.num_qubits, circ.num_clbits), pass clbits to append, copy global_phase.
